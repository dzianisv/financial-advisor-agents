### IV. SIZING SURVIVABLE TO -50%

Housel's position sizing principle (`05-getting-vs-keeping-wealth.md`): "Size so a wide range of outcomes is survivable." The specific math here is: for any position to survive a -50% drawdown without forcing a behavioral failure (panic sale, margin call, or portfolio ruin), it must be sized such that the -50% loss represents a tolerable fraction of the total portfolio.

**The practical rule from the brief:**

If -50% on a position would represent ≥10% of total portfolio loss, the position is too large for a behaviorally survivable entry. Because if the 10% loss threshold triggers panic behavior (which the research on behavioral finance shows it will for most people), you will sell at the bottom — converting a temporary loss into a permanent one and breaking the compounding clock.

**Per-asset survivable sizing ceiling (given -50% scenario):**

**On the question itself — "find stocks that will surge soon":**

**On "overlooked, undervalued":**

The more useful question is: which of these names have business quality that justifies a long holding period through a -50% drawdown? That question produces different answers than "which will surge soon."

**Room for error reminder:**
Housel assumes his own lifetime returns will be ⅓ lower than historical averages and saves more accordingly. Apply this to AI infrastructure: assume the AI capex cycle delivers ⅓ less than current forecasts. Which of these 7 names is still a good business at that scenario? RMBS (80% gross margins, IP licensing) and PLAB (duopoly, long customer qualification cycles) are likely still viable businesses. AEHR at $45M revenue and near-zero earnings is not.
